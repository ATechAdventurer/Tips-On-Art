"Create Dreams" Youth Summer Camp 2013
for ART::MIND::COMMUNITY

TIPS On Art is proud to produce the fourth seies of multi-disciplinary art summer day camp for youth, designed to facilitate their contribution to the creative community of Austin. TIPS On Art exists to inspire our next generation of artists and creative thinkers.



Camp activities will include collage, drawing, sculpting, painting, mosaic puzzles, music, photography, movement, jewelry making, drumming, journaling, and storytelling- with a focus on applying Sustainable Practices with Upcycling Materials and Healthy Eating.

Campers will get to meet and work directly with local guest artists to get inspired and practice new techniques. Each week, the outcome of a group art project will be combined with other camp weeks for a comprehensive piece to be exhibited at IIDA�s Annual Evolution Community Fund Raiser, whose partnering again this year with the nationwide ZeroLandfill�.



Project Zero- Landfill is a grassroots upcycling or reuse program started in Cleveland, Ohio in 2006. The ZeroLandfill� Austin project team assists the architectural and interior design community in identifying, diverting from local landfills, and re-purposing back into the community thousands of pounds of specification samples that hold value for other audiences, like artists and educators. After the exhibit, the camp-created artwork will be donated to another Austin non-profit to inspire creative community thinking.

Kids can work towards Girl Scout Badge Work, Boy Scout�Art Activity Pins, and Campfire Creative Emblems!

All campers will receive a souvenir t-shirt.
